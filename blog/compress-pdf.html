Compress your PDF file for free, directly in your browser. No upload, no account, no limits.

Why compress a PDF?

Large PDF files are hard to share by email, slow to load, and take up unnecessary storage. Compressing a PDF reduces its file size while keeping its content readable — text, bookmarks, and links are fully preserved.

Step-by-step guide

1

Choose your compression level

iWorkPDF offers three levels: Max compression (smallest file, lower image quality), Recommended (balanced — best for most use cases), and Light (minimal compression, best visual quality).

2

Instant size estimate

Before you compress, iWorkPDF scans your PDF to estimate how much it can be reduced. PDFs with embedded images can typically be reduced by 40–70%.

3

Download instantly

Your compressed PDF is generated locally in your browser and downloaded immediately. Nothing is uploaded to any server.

Tips

  • PDFs that are already highly compressed may not reduce much further.
  • If your PDF contains mainly text and vector graphics, the gain will be smaller than for image-heavy PDFs.
  • For the best balance between size and quality, use the "Recommended" setting.

FAQ

Does compressing a PDF lose quality?

Only image quality is reduced. Text, bookmarks, internal links, and document structure are fully preserved.

Is there a file size limit?

No. iWorkPDF runs entirely in your browser and is limited only by your device's RAM.
